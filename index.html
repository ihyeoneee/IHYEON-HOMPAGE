Research

Gerontological Education & Learning Habitus

This research examines how older adults form their learning identities and habitus from a generational perspective, situating aging within the broader context of lifelong education. Drawing on Bourdieu's concept of habitus, it investigates how historical experiences — particularly those of the Liberation Generation and Korean War Generation in Korea and Germany — shape learning orientations, dispositions, and participation patterns across the life course. The comparative lens between Korea and Germany provides insight into how national history and social structure influence intergenerational learning.

Ph.D. Dissertation (2023) Frontiers in Education (2023)

Adult Informal Learning

This line of research investigates the processes, structures, and influencing factors of informal learning among adult learners. Moving beyond formal educational settings, it explores how adults naturally acquire knowledge, skills, and values through everyday experience. A heuristic model of informal learning is developed and applied to understand the self-directed and context-embedded nature of adult learning, with attention to how personal motivation, social environment, and prior experiences converge in non-formal learning processes.

Theory and Practice of Education (2024) Journal of Adult and Continuing Education (2017)

AI & Digital Competency in Education

This research develops and validates frameworks for AI-integrated education and digital competency, with particular focus on how teachers should cultivate AI-digital competencies across different career stages. Using Delphi methodology and career life cycle analysis, it constructs stage-specific competency indicators that are responsive to evolving technological demands in education. The work also encompasses systematic reviews of AI utilization in arts and culture education, mapping the current state of the field and identifying future research directions.

Frontiers in Education (2026, SCOPUS) Korean Journal of Educational Research (2025) Journal of Educational Research (2026)

Lifelong Education & Cultural Learning

This research explores the intersection of traditional cultural practices and lifelong education, using Korean tea ceremony (다도) as a lens to examine how cultural participation functions as a meaningful form of adult learning. The transition to online participation during and after COVID-19 is also examined, revealing how digital platforms reshape the experiential dimensions of cultural learning. This work raises broader questions about the role of embodied, aesthetic experience in lifelong education and the challenges of mediating such experiences through technology.

Journal of Tea Culture and Industry (2022) Journal of Tea Culture and Industry (2020)

Generation Theory & Social Mobility in Education

This strand of research critically examines generational theory — particularly Mannheim's foundational work — and explores its implications for adult education. It interrogates the conceptual boundaries of "generation" as an analytical category and investigates how generational identity shapes educational participation and outcomes. The research also extends to transnational contexts, examining how cultural capital mediates the educational mobility of Asian middle-class students studying in Germany, illuminating the interplay between class, nationality, and educational aspiration.
